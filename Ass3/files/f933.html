China's enterprises face heavy tax burden

09-24-2013 09:44 BJT

Text:A A A | Email
Share |

Chinese enterprises face a heavy tax burden. That’s according to a new report released by China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology. Besides a heavy tax burden, the report also highlights several other problematic sticking points in China’s current corporate tax landscape.

If one takes a look at the top lines of Chinese enterprises, they do quite well in revenues. According to a new tax report by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the companies surveyed have an average profit margin of 19%, a solid figure before taxes are taken into account. However, looking at the profit margin after taxes, the companies surveyed showed an average of just a little over 5%.

What’s more, the report also shows that there are imbalances in tax burden levels across different regions in China. Enterprises in the eastern part of the country bear less burden compared to those in central and western China.

In tackling these pressing challenges, policy makers have introduced more tax breaks and career training subsidies. However, according to the report, many of these policies failed to meet expectations. For example, less than 20% of Chinese companies are actually utilizing career training subsidies provided by the government. Analysts say, there’s still a ways to go for China to develop a more efficient corporate tax scheme.
