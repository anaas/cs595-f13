What is an Engineer Resume?

Some engineers wrongly assume that a standard resume will be enough to land an engineer job. Engineering resumes are however pretty distinctive, in terms of structure and content.

Contrary to generic resumes that must follow a chronological order, engineer resumes should put an emphasis on skills, achievements and projects.

In addition, since most Engineering recruiters use scanning software to sort and save resumes, your resume must include a lot of industry buzzwords.


Why a Professional Engineering Resume Writer should write your Resume

With years of experience writing engineering resumes and the right certification, professional resume writers know how to write an engineering resume. They are familiar with the latest employment trends and techniques and will use these guidelines to write your resume.

Not only will they customize your resume to your field — electrical, computer, mechanical, design, network, project, civil, chemical, software, aerospace, etc.. — but they will include all the industry buzzwords Engineering recruiters are looking for. Your new resume will become an innovative combo resume, blending the best of functional and chronological so that your talents, project management, areas of expertise and career milestones are all showcased without burying your work history and company loyalty.
