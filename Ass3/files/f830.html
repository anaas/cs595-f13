Dovish Fed Hits Dollar and Other Top Forex News.

By simongray on Sep 23, 2013 07:00:40 GMT

  • Tweet
  • Email
  • Tweet
  • Email

The U.S. dollar started the week off mixed on Monday after comments made by New York Federal Reserve President William Dudley weakened the dollar against the yen and a host of other currencies.

Dudley, speaking this morning, defended last week’s decision by the Fed to keep its monetary stimulus program unchanged. Saying, two key tapering tests had not yet been met.

After the statement the dollar fell to session lows against the yen, but recovered later in the session. USD/JPY finally closed down 0.52% to 98.86.

Meanwhile in Europe, ECB President Mario Draghi told the Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs in Brussels, that the central bank is ready to launch a third round of Long Term Refinancing Operations if necessary.

The statement came after German Chancellor Angela Merkel won an historic third term in the German elections. Her conservative bloc got 41.5% of the vote – their best result since 1994, but just short of a clear majority. She will now have to seek to form a coalition, most likely with the centre-left Social Democrats (SPD) – but only after hard bargaining.

In economic news, official data showed that manufacturing output in the eurozone was weaker than expected this month, but the service sector showed a small improvement.

The euro fell to session lows against the dollar, with EUR/USD closing down 0.19% to 1.3498.

In the U.K. the pound pulled back slightly from session highs against the dollar, with GBP/USD rising 0.28% to 1.6050, down from a session high of 1.6072. The dollar regained some lost ground after Dudley’s comments.

Elsewhere, the dollar lost ground against the New Zealand and Canadian dollars. With, NZD/USD edging up 0.08% to 0.8381 and USD/CAD sliding 0.28% to 1.0278.

The Australian dollar was boosted after strong data from China showed that the preliminary reading of China’s HSBC manufacturing index rose to a six-month high of 51.2 in September, up from 50.1 in August.

AUD/USD closed the session up 0.55% to 0.9448

More coverage of today’s top story.

  • Dean Popplewell: EUR prefers to watch the Fed speakers. – It comes as no surprise that Chancellor Merkel’s Christian Democratic bloc took +41.5% compared to +25.7% for the Social Democrats in yesterday’s German federal election, according to results from all 299 districts. However, this leaves her short of a majority and needing a coalition partner to govern Europe’s biggest economy. Now it’s back to the negotiation table to figure out who is best prepared to deliver the goods to form a coalition. The European markets have shrugged off the German vote – the focus remains on the U.S. and its economic data.
  • FT: U.S. economy not ready for tapering, says Fed official. – The US economy does not have enough momentum to justify a slowing of Federal Reserve asset purchases, according to the most senior Fed policy maker to speak since the central bank surprised investors by deciding not to taper its bond buying last week.

More Top Stories:

Marc Chandler: Great Graphic: S&P 500 and the euro. – The relationship between the dollar and the stock market continues to be closely followed by investors and observers. The rub is that one cannot simply look at the dollar, but must look at the dollar relative to another currency or currencies. The euro-dollar exchange rate is the most actively traded currency pair, and we look at that relationship to the S&P 500.

Bloomberg: Canada dollar gains first time in three days before retail sales. – The Canadian dollar gained for the first time in three days before a report tomorrow forecast to show retail sales rebounded in July from the worst monthly decline this year.

Bloomberg: Aussie dollar rises as China manufacturing data exceed estimates. – Australia’s currency climbed against 15 of its 16 major counterparts after data on China’s manufacturing surpassed economist estimates, boosting prospects for the South Pacific nation’s commodity exports.

TVNZ: NZ dollar little changed after upbeat Chinese manufacturing. – The New Zealand dollar was little changed in local trading after Chinese manufacturing figures beat expectations, as investors rethink last week’s action when the Federal Reserve didn’t embark on slowing down its pace of printing money.

Reuters: Euro under pressure on German coalition concerns. – The euro weakened against most currencies on Monday, hurt by worries about how long it will take Angela Merkel to form a coalition after her party’s victory in Sunday’s German election.

Tim Iacono: The Fed surprise and A weaker dollar could be very good for gold. – Gold and silver prices rose and then fell on news from the Federal Reserve last week and this process is likely to both continue and intensify now that the central bank has surprised markets by delaying the start of tapering its $85 billion per month money printing effort. After the Fed policy decision was announced on Wednesday, the gold price saw its biggest one-day move higher since June 2009, but most of those gains were given back on Friday when a central bank official suggested that tapering could come as soon as next month.

Investing.com: EUR/USD Consolidates after 7-month high. – Last weeks EUR/USD breakout, which rose swiftly above the last major high of 1.3450 occurred mid-week around Wednesday’s Fed announcement. Price action for the remainder of last week subsequently showed little to no follow-through on that substantial move, and the pair has now formed a small, flag-like consolidation pattern just above major support/resistance at the breached 1.3500 level.

MarketWatch: Gold futures extend losses to a second session. – Gold futures closed lower Monday, extending the steep drop from the previous session as concerns that the U.S. Federal Reserve will taper its bond-buying program remained on the table.
