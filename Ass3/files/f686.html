Celebrity home feature: Mark Wahlberg

Credit: hiltonhyland.com

As the everyman actor, Mark Wahlberg's undeniable charm on screen while portraying realistic characters in unrealistic situations has carved him a nice niche in the entertainment industry.

A far cry from his short stint in boy band, New Kids on the Block and as a rapper, Wahlberg got to where he is through hard work and perseverance.

It's no surprise then, that some of that hard work was manifested in the beautiful Mediterranean-style home he currently owns.

Although he has been trying to sell it so he can move into a larger compound to accommodate his growing family, it was the first property the ‘Ted' star ever owned.  

He purchased the 9,000 square foot property in November 2001 for US$4.95 million.

Originally, it came with seven bedrooms and 10 bathrooms. Further renovations to the sprawling estate gave it a gourmet chef's kitchen, a movie screen room and a guest house.

The outdoor space has a sports court, a putting green, an outdoor pool with waterfall, cabanas and a grotto spa.

Credit: hauteliving.com

Also, true to his fitness lifestyle, Wahlberg had a 2,500 square foot detached gym with a full size boxing ring built on his property.

The gym is as fully stocked with equipment as any of the mega gyms. The only difference is that it's completely private. 

Although the house is located in affluent Beverly Hills, it is quoted by Wahlberg as being ‘secluded' with a ‘view that looks out across the canyon' and has amazing sunsets.

Wahlberg further expounds that it is peaceful and relaxing with convenient access to the city.

He first listed it at almost US$16 million in 2008 but was unsuccessful in getting a buyer.

In 2012, he slashed the price considerably to $12.995 million.

This marks the third time in four years that Wahlberg has tried to sell his property, which incidentally has Demi Moore as a neighbour. 

Credit: celebritynetworth.com

It is a beautiful house no doubt but the difficulty the 'Pain & Gain' star is facing in selling is shared among his celebrity counterparts.

The houses are so lavishly designed but so specifically customized to their respective styles and preferences that the potential buyer may end up planning renovation costs on top of the sale price.

It's a never ending cycle too as renovations and remodelling would only drive the cost up further, making it harder to sell.

The house does have one thing going for it - other than being remarkably gorgeous. It's seclusion and sprawling grounds provide much needed privacy from prying paparazzi eyes.

If that's not a selling point for A-list celebrities, then we don't know what is. 

Written by: Christopher Chitty
